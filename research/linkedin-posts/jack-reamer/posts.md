Once I get 25K+ impressions a week, inbound LI leads will start booking calls with me on autopilot."

I used to think that. *Myth busted.

Turns out:

A) LI content matters, but it’s a marathon.
An impression is just an impression. The magic is building recognition, reciprocity, and trust over time.

B) If you want more calls now, don’t wait for inbound.
Instead, review who’s engaging with your content and matches your ICP… then reach out first. 

Try something like: "Thanks for checking out the post. {Personal Note on their company/industry/role/posts/etc.} Anything I can help with when it comes to {solving pain}?"

Some teams have a lot of success when they just pick up the phone and call this short list directly. 

Bottom line: Content creates the signal. Outreach converts it.

---

**Why relevant:** Reframes content marketing as a signal-generation tool rather than a standalone lead source — introduces the "content creates signal, outreach converts it" framework where LinkedIn engagement data feeds directly into a warm outreach list, a high-leverage tactic for any B2B SaaS pipeline.

## Post 2

**Date:** 2025-12
**URL:** https://salesbread.com/cold-email-cadence/

---

Fact: According to data from 1 million cold emails, 57.2% of replies come after your first follow-up email.

So if you're not sending lead follow-ups, you'll miss out on the majority of your opportunities.

Here's the follow-up cadence we use at SalesBread:
- Email 1: Day 1
- Email 2: Day 4 (first follow-up — this is where most replies happen)
- Email 3: Day 8
- Email 4: Day 14
- Email 5: Day 21

The key: each follow-up should reference a NEW angle, not just "just checking in." Change the pain point, the case study, or the CTA.

If someone is a high-value prospect, we'll follow up between 5 and 12 times. Less for broad lists. More precision for tight ICPs.

---

**Why relevant:** Data-backed follow-up cadence framework (57.2% of replies come after the first follow-up) — directly usable for B2B SaaS teams structuring their email sequences and deciding how many touches to send.

## Post 3

**Date:** 2025-ongoing
**URL:** https://salesbread.com/linkedin-outreach-agency/

---

Most of your website visitors aren't ready to buy. But that doesn't mean they're not interested.

The CCQ method for LinkedIn outreach (what we use at SalesBread):
- C: Compliment — Reference something specific about their profile or recent post
- C: Commonality — Find a genuine shared connection, industry, or experience
- Q: Question — Ask one specific, non-salesy question that invites a response

Example connection request:
"[Name] — noticed you recently posted about scaling outbound without adding headcount. We've been working through the same problem. Would love to connect and exchange notes."

No pitch. No ask. Just a reason to connect that's relevant to them.

After connection: follow up within 48 hours with something of value — a resource, a data point, a relevant case study. Then ask for the meeting.

---

**Why relevant:** The CCQ (Compliment, Commonality, Question) framework gives a concrete, repeatable structure for LinkedIn cold outreach that avoids the "pitch slap" most prospects ignore — directly applicable for any B2B SaaS multichannel pipeline.
