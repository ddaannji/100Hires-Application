The 7 Elements of a Perfect Cold Email (Show Me You Know Me® framework):

1. Subject line — Research-based, hyper-specific. E.g. "Switzerland + liip cheeseburger + [Your Company]" — information only the prospect would recognize.
2. Opening line — "We've yet to be properly introduced, but I'm Sam McKenna, CEO of #samsales." Short, human, no pitch.
3. Transition — One authentic tie-in to the opener that leads naturally to your value prop.
4. Value proposition — Focus on the specific challenge you solve, not what your product does. "If you're anything like our clients, you see your usage rates of LinkedIn Sales Navigator hover around 5% of your licenses."
5. Social proof — One concise proof point, not a list of logos.
6. CTA — Specific, low-commitment ask. Not "let's hop on a call" but something easier to say yes to.
7. Signature — Clean, professional, no distracting links.

You've done your research, you've put together the perfect Show Me You Know Me email, you're ready to fire away, but...when should you send it and what happens after that? 

1. Do all your research M-W and save your 1st emails in your cadences in draft, aiming for 50 net new emails

2. Fire them off on Thursday and Friday.

3. Email two should come no more than 48 hours after email one, and should contain no new information; this one is just a follow up. You've already (presumably) done a killer job with email one, so remind them of it vs. trying to sell them again.

3a. This means that email two comes out on Sat and Sun, when they're already on their phones, checking emails, working an avg. of 7 hours (HBR), and have less emails that compete with yours.

4. Send email three next Wednesday PM, this one with another challenge they likely face.

5. LI connection request on Friday with this script:

Sam, my name might look familiar as I've sent you a few emails. I'd be grateful for the chance to chat with you about how (company) could be of help to hashtag#samsales - if you're ever up for a quick conversation, just say the word.

*pls note removal of "I'd love to" <--ditch the salesy language every chance you get.

6. Your execs inboxes are flooded with connection requests that are blank - yours will stand out simply because you made the effort. 

7. Bubble Hunt - see the word "conversation" in your connection? Pop in to LI on a Sat AM or Sun PM, search for that word in your DMs, which should go to all of your super high level prospects, and filter all messages that have included that word. Then, see who has green filled in bubbles next to their names, which means they're online, and send them a DM with a value-driven nurture or a SMYKM on something you've read/listened to that they recently published.
