Loom CTAs will get you more positive replies. They'll also convert to calls at a lower rate

I see this a lot of campaigns - you make the CTA

"can I send you a quick video?"

...and the replies look great.

20, 30 interested people wanting a Loom. But then only a handful of those actually convert into calls.

The main reason is that a Loom CTA is a soft ask. It's easy to say yes to.

"Sure, send me a video" costs the prospect nothing. No calendar commitment, no time block, no obligation.

So you get a ton of people who are mildly curious but not serious enough to actually get on a call.

If your CTA is pushing for a call directly (or even just "want me to send over some more info?"), fewer people respond. But the ones who do are already closer to booking.

Neither is wrong. It depends on your situation.

If you're doing it for yourself and you have the time to record Looms & nurture those leads through to a call, it can work well.

If you're running it at scale or doing it for clients, the complexity adds up fast. You're recording custom videos for 20+ people and maybe 3 of them book. That math gets rough.

I run a cold email agency across ~30 clients. We don't use Loom CTAs for most client client campaigns for that exact reason.

Too many moving parts when you're coordinating with someone else's team (and you're banking on someone recording a video)

But I still tell people: split test it. Run a Loom CTA against a direct CTA and look at meetings booked, not just reply rate.

**Why relevant:** Tactical CTA optimization insight — shows the trade-off between Loom video CTAs (higher replies, lower call conversion) vs direct call asks, helping B2B SaaS teams choose the right CTA based on their pipeline goal.
