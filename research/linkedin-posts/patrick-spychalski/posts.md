## Post 1

**Date:** 2024-Q4

Clay has been slowly de-ranking automated outbound as one of its core use cases. Here's why I think it's a good idea:

As background, for everyone who's been in the Clay-verse for a while, you know that the main use case in the beginning was being able to send automated emails/messages at scale. 

I would argue that it's still incredible at doing this- we do it every day for our clients with great results. 

However, the ability to do this successfully is becoming more and more difficult for the average user. Deliverability issues, copy shortcomings, and other factors get in the way of success- then people blame Clay for it. 

The problem is almost certainly not Clay, it's your strategy, and with cold outbound becoming more commoditized, it's tough to have a good strategy. 

Use cases like CRM enrichment, TAM sourcing, and lead scoring are much less trivial for Clay, and when you're trying to build a $10B company, you want to focus on the things that EVERYONE can do well with the product, not just the upper echelon of subject matter experts. 

On top of that, most companies don't want to shift from the SDR model, especially on the enterprise side. Automating something like CRM enrichment is far simpler to demo, and still a massive value add. 

In short, I think it's the right move, and we've been offering tons of inbound, CRM, and scoring based stuff over at The Kiln as a result. 

Also, you should still absolutely use it for automated outbound. Like, it works. 

Interested to hear other people's thoughts on this!

---

**Why relevant:** Challenges the "more data = better results" assumption that dominates Clay discourse — argues that simplicity and signal quality beat complexity, which directly shapes how B2B SaaS teams should architect their outbound systems.

You should use Clay for inbound. Here's why:

While outbound is a great use case within Clay, their inbound use cases are arguably stronger. 

Clay can act as an aggregator for all of your inbound marketing data, which is all automatically routed, enriched, and then sent to:
- Your sales team to act upon the new leads
- Your CRM with newly enriched Clay data
- An automated sequencing tool like Smartlead, HeyReach, or La Growth Machine. 

A major problem within essentially any mid-market/enterprise business running a mature marketing/sales system is the ability to verify, standardize, and route data easily. Clay can do all of that. 

I made a quick video outlining this idea- we've been doing this for clients at The Kiln and the value add has been pretty massive 🚀

**Why relevant:** Expands the cold outreach pipeline concept to include inbound-triggered sequences — shows how the same Clay infrastructure used for outbound can compress response time and improve conversion on warm leads.

New to Clay? Here are three essential use cases that I would recommend trying:

1. Automated or Semi-Automated Outbound 
The three main outbound use cases that we come across at The Kiln are a) completely automated outreach, b) pre-drafted copy for sdrs/bdrs to review/edit prior to sending, and c) an automated research workflow that allow for sdrs/bdrs to focus on outbound without needing to do research themselves. Regardless of which you decide to choose, the flow looks pretty much the same.

2. Inbound Lead Enrichment/Scoring
Dealing with inbound leads can get annoying, because inevitably the data payload from each source varies in content and format. Clay solves the formatting problem, and you can turn a bunch of unstructured data into a unified lead source. Then, you can use their unreal enrichment capabilities to score inbound leads to figure out whether they're worth engaging or not. 

3. CRM Enrichment 
To this day, Clay's most valuable use case across the board imo. Every company with a CRM should have this, and it solves so many data integrity problems I couldn't even fit them in the post. It turns a messy, stale CRM into a constantly updated and enriched source of truth, which is what it was supposed to be in the first place. Can't recommend doing this enough- it's been a gamechanger for so many companies already. 

Interested to hear if people would add any others to this list of essentials! ⬇️

---

**Why relevant:** Practical entry point for teams new to Clay — shows the three highest-ROI starting points so B2B SaaS teams don't get overwhelmed by the platform's complexity and actually ship campaigns.
